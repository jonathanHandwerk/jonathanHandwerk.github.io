Second Entry

Nothing special, Aug ?, 2020

I remember teaching a small group of students about the history of the Oregon Trail (the topic came up somewhat randomly, I am guessing, while explaining my West Coast origins). They were beginners at English. I began explaining what the Oregon Trail was and quickly realized I was just describing what I learned from the video game. But I ran with it. Being a very basic video game, it is probably good template for an explanation of the Oregon Trail in basic English. I explained how people traveled in a wagon, sometimes the wagon broke, members would get sick. They would use bullets to hunt for deer. Sometimes you have to cross a river and everybody in your group would die. Having played the game, this was all very vivid to me. But to my listeners, maybe it was kind of an empty explanation. Basically just me reminiscing and realizing that all of my knowledge of the Oregon Trail came from that video game. Or maybe compared to anything I was told or taught by a teacher, the memories created by the game were much stronger.
